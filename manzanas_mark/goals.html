Goals

My Goals

  1. Maintain a High GWA:

    - I aim to maintain a strong grade weighted average (GWA) throughout my college career. A high GPA can open up more opportunities and demonstrates my commitment to excellence

  2. Expand My Technical Skills:

    - I will continuously expand my technical skills by learning new programming languages, software, and technologies relevant to my field of study. Staying up to date with the latest industry trends is essential in IT.

  3. Build a Portfolio:

    - I'm going to create a portfolio of projects, including personal coding projects, applications, or websites. A strong portfolio can be a valuable asset when seeking internships or jobs.

  4. Participate in Internships:

    - I'll seek out internships in my field of interest. Internships provide real-world experience, help me make industry connections, and improve my resume.

  5. Stay Informed:

    - I'll stay updated on industry news and emerging technologies. I will subscribe to tech blogs, attend seminars, and consider certifications to enhance my knowledge and make myself more competitive in the job market.

Reflection:

This past few months has been a very interesting experience for me. For the three subjects, Web Development, Systems Integration and IT Elective, there were a lot of requirements that we needed to accomplish which means that there are also a lot of new and different things that we must learn in order to complete them. Every week there were two things that was always on my mind, and I think most of my classmates too. These were the recitation that we'd have before the start and the 1 hour hands on activity at the end of every Web Development class. I'd try my best to prepare for both but at first I was a bit overwhelemed because there were so many new information that we needed to learn and understand. The more time that passed the more I got accustomed to it. I'd sleep during Friday afternoon and wake up at around midnight so that I'd have time to wait for the presentation for the class tomorrow in order to do some advanced reading. Me and my classmates would also study during break time at the library before the start of our Web Development class.

I experienced a lot stress while working on the different tasks we were assigned, like the project and the exercises. I also experienced great satisfaction whenever I am able to overcome the problems and challenges that I've encountered while coding. I'd spend hours and sometimes even days just trying so figure out why a certain part of my code doesn't work and nothing beats the I never expected it to be easy, but I also didn't have an idea on how difficult it would be. satisfaction I felt whenever I was able to overcome them. Now knowing what it's like in the IT industry, I have no regrets and still intend on continuing my career in IT because the more difficult something is, the higher the amount of value that is held within it.

5 years from now I see myself working as a game developer working at a game development company. I've always loved playing video games ever since I was little. Nothing beats the joy I felt during those times. It is also one of the reasons why I chose IT so that I can learn how a video game is developed. My plan is to combine my skills in coding and passion for video games to create educational video games that students of any age can benefit from. There a already a lot of existing educational games although there are things you can learn from them, the majority of those types of games are very boring and not fun enough to convince a child to continue playing them. My plan is to find the right balance between the 2 of being educational and still being very enjoyable to play. I got This idea because when I play games I also consider what the game can help me develop. Like for example rhythm games help develop my reaction time and hand eye coordination. Puzzle games help me think outside the box. Strategy games help develop my problem solving skills and many more. I chose IT because I know that there is a lot of money that can be made under this profession and support my family, but I would also like to be able to help other people with my coding skills and expertise.
